# Astra Microwave Products Limited

Astra Microwave Products Limited designs, develops and manufactures RF (radio frequency) and microwave sub-systems and super-components used in defence, space, meteorology and civil communications. Incorporated in 1991 and listed on the BSE (532493) and NSE (ASTRAMICRO), the company has contributed to 35 radars, 25 satellites and roughly 15 strategic programmes to date. As of 10 July 2026 it had a market capitalization of approximately ₹17,125 crore (screener.in).

## Value Chain Positioning

Astra sits as a sub-system/super-component supplier one tier below India's large defence platform integrators and government agencies — the specific upstream raw-material and electronic-component base was not detailed in the sources reviewed for this report. Downstream, its customers and counterparties (as named across its Q3 FY26 and Q4 FY26 concalls) include DRDO (the qualifying authority for its radar programmes), Bharat Electronics Limited (BEL), Hindustan Aeronautics Limited (HAL, in negotiation for the Uttam AESA radar and executing Su-30 Virupaksha/Angad upgrade work), and ISRO/India's space programme. Exports run through Astra Rafael Comsys (ARC), a joint venture/subsidiary with Rafael Advanced Defense Systems (Israel), which co-develops the RF portion of software-defined radios (SDRs) rather than the lower-value, offset-based export work Astra did historically.

## Medium Term (6 to 12 Months)

- **FY27 Revenue Guidance** `[Pending]`: Management has planned standalone sales of ₹1,300-1,400 crore for FY27 (~15-20% YoY growth over FY26's ₹1,157 crore), split roughly 40% R&D and 60% production-area orders. "planned to book sales of INR1300 crores to INR1400 crores, that is around 15% to 20% growth compared to FY26, covering 40% of R&D and 60% from the production area"

- **ARC (JV) FY27 Growth Target** `[Pending]`: The JV/subsidiary Astra Rafael Comsys is guided to grow order booking and sales by a minimum of 50% in FY27, with sales expected to cross ₹600 crore versus ₹360 crore in FY26. "we expect the minimum growth of 50% both in terms of order booking and as well as sales. As on date, we have visibility of INR200 crores order book in FY27 for ARC and also expected to cross INR600 crores sales"

## Long Term (1+ Years)

- **Multi-Year Revenue Tripling Target** `[On Track]`: First flagged on the Q3 FY26 call as ~₹8,000-10,000 crore of new order booking and ~₹7,500 crore-plus of cumulative sales over four years, and reiterated on the Q4 FY26 call as an expectation to nearly triple FY26's turnover within 4.5-5.5 years (i.e. by roughly FY30-31). "MV Reddy and SG Reddy are very confident of giving us a number of around INR8,000 crores to INR10,000 crores worth of new order booking and concurrent sales happening to the tune of about INR7,500 crores plus over the next 4 years themselves" (Q3 FY26); "structurally positioned to nearly triple its turnover over this period" (Q4 FY26)

- **Higher-Value Export Mix via ARC/Rafael Co-Development** `[Pending]`: Exports are shifting from older, lower-value offset-based work to RF co-development with Rafael on software-defined radios, which management says carries materially higher value-add. "these are the things where the RF portion of the SDRs are being co-developed between Rafael and Astra... the value add is much higher than the regular exports that were recorded"

- **Uttam AESA Radar DRDO Qualification** `[Delayed]`: The timeline for DRDO to qualify the Uttam AESA radar has slipped — from a "hopeful within 6 months" comment on the Q3 FY26 call (implying ~August 2026) to "FY28" on the Q4 FY26 call. "in maybe FY28, DRDO will be ready to qualify the overall radar" / "next FY28 I think radar should be get qualified"

## Marquee & Niche Customers

- **DRDO**: The qualifying/certifying authority and customer for Astra's flagship radar programmes, including QRSAM and the Uttam AESA radar. (Source: Q3 FY26 and Q4 FY26 concalls)
- **Bharat Electronics Limited (BEL)**: Counterparty for a QRSAM-linked contract expected around June 2026. (Source: Q4 FY26 concall, 27 May 2026)
- **Hindustan Aeronautics Limited (HAL)**: In negotiation for the Uttam radar order (expected Q2/Q3 FY27); also the platform integrator for the Su-30 Virupaksha and Su-30 Angad upgrade programmes Astra supplies into. (Source: Q4 FY26 concall, 27 May 2026)
- **ISRO / India's space programme**: Named directly in the Q4 FY26 concall as an order source, consistent with the company's disclosed contribution to 25 satellites to date. (Source: Q4 FY26 concall, 27 May 2026; company profile)
- **Astra Rafael Comsys (ARC)**: Joint venture/subsidiary with Rafael Advanced Defense Systems (Israel) — the vehicle for RF-co-developed SDR exports. (Source: Q4 FY26 concall, 27 May 2026)

No customer-concentration percentage (e.g. "top 5/10 customers = X% of revenue") was disclosed in the sources reviewed. **Customers' own guidance**: DRDO, BEL, HAL and ISRO are government/PSU entities that do not publish the kind of forward capacity or demand guidance a private-sector OEM customer might; no independently disclosed guidance was found for any of them in the sources reviewed for this report.

## Industry Tailwinds / Headwinds

**Tailwinds**

- **Rising defence capex and localisation push**: India's defence capital spending rose an estimated 22% for FY26-27, and the revised Defence Acquisition Procedure (DAP) 2026 pushes localisation of radar sub-systems for indigenous aircraft — a direct tailwind for RF/microwave sub-system suppliers. (Source: sector coverage, Feb-Jul 2026)
- **Rising DRDO R&D funding for AESA radar technology**: reported up 12.4% in the FY26-27 budget, supporting the technology roadmap Astra's own AESA radar programme depends on. (Source: sector coverage, Jan 2026)
- **Broad-based sector order inflow**: peers including BEL, HAL, Bharat Dynamics and Zen Technologies are all reporting strong order inflows, indicating a sector-wide, multi-year order-book cycle rather than an Astra-specific phenomenon. (Source: sector coverage, Feb-Jul 2026)

**Headwinds**

- **Execution, not demand, is the binding constraint**: sector commentary describes an "order book-loaded defence sector" that now needs an "execution pick-up," i.e. delivery and manufacturing throughput risk rather than order-intake risk. (Source: Business Standard, 8 Jul 2026)
- **Government/DPSU programme timelines are structurally prone to slippage** — directly evidenced by Astra's own DRDO radar-qualification delay (see Long Term outlook above), a feature of the defence-order business generally rather than company-specific mismanagement.

## Key Risks

- **Programme-execution/timeline risk**: The Uttam AESA radar's DRDO qualification has already slipped from a "hopeful within 6 months" comment (Feb 2026) to "FY28" (May 2026) — a concrete instance of the sector-wide execution risk noted in Industry Headwinds, and a direct dependency for the ₹7,500cr+ four-year sales target.
- **Very low, declining promoter holding**: Promoter shareholding has fallen from 15.80% (Mar 2017) to 6.54% (Mar 2026) — among the lower promoter stakes for a company of this scale — with no disclosed explanation for the multi-year decline found in the sources reviewed.
- **Pending warrant dilution**: ₹130.50cr (75%) of the June 2025 promoter warrant issue remains unconverted, with a conversion deadline of 29 December 2026; full conversion would add further equity dilution.
- **Demerger execution uncertainty**: The Space/Meteorology/Hydrology demerger into ASTPL is only board-approved in-principle and subject to shareholder/regulatory/NCLT sign-off, with post-demerger standalone economics for either entity not yet disclosed.
- **Programme concentration**: Management itself frames the multi-year growth target as resting on "just five to six programs" (QRSAM, Uttam radar, Su-30 Virupaksha, Su-30 Angad, JV/ARC business, and electronic mines) — a relatively narrow set of large, discrete defence programmes whose individual timing/award risk each carry outsized weight.
