# OBSC Perfection Limited

OBSC Perfection Limited manufactures precision metal components, primarily supplying original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) and Tier 1 suppliers in the automotive sector, alongside a growing non-automotive business in defence, marine and telecommunication infrastructure. Incorporated in 2017 and converted to a public limited company on 12 July 2024, the company listed on the NSE SME Emerge platform on 29 October 2024 following an IPO that raised ₹66.02 crore. As of 10 July 2026 it had a market capitalization of approximately ₹1,720 crore (screener.in). The company is part of the Anglian Omega Group, whose promoter-group heritage traces back to a bright-bar factory in Faridabad, Haryana, founded in 1969 — a promoter-group historical fact distinct from OBSC Perfection's own current manufacturing footprint (see Manufacturing Locations below).

## Value Chain Positioning

OBSC Perfection machines precision metal components from raw metal/bar stock inputs (specific suppliers not disclosed in the sources reviewed) and supplies both directly to OEMs and indirectly via Tier 1 suppliers. Named automotive customers/relationships include Ashok Leyland, Tata Motors, and Mahindra (the latter via Tier 1 supplier Tenneco), with Tata AutoComp cited as a fast-growing customer with Tesla-related automotive exposure. In the non-automotive business, the company supplies defence, marine and telecommunications infrastructure manufacturers, and is diversifying further into humanoid robotics (currently at the prototyping/validation stage with customers) and aerospace (pursuing AS9100D certification).

## Industry Tailwinds / Headwinds

**Tailwinds**

- **Automotive OEM/Tier-1 demand growth**: reflected in the ~82% automotive share of the order book and the FY26 revenue acceleration (+53.8% YoY).
- **Defence/marine/telecom infrastructure diversification**: a structurally different, less-cyclical demand base than pure automotive component supply, already ~19% of the order book.
- **Emerging humanoid robotics and aerospace verticals**: both cited by management as future growth avenues, though pre-revenue-scale as of this report (prototyping/validation stage for robotics; certification still in progress for aerospace) — genuine option value, not yet a proven revenue driver.

**Headwinds**

- **SME-platform listing**: OBSC Perfection trades on the NSE SME Emerge platform rather than the mainboard, which typically carries different liquidity, disclosure-cadence and institutional-ownership characteristics than mainboard-listed peers covered elsewhere in this report series — a structural factor for readers to weigh, not a company-specific operating risk.
- **Concentration in automotive component supply**: despite diversification efforts, ~82% of the current order book remains automotive-linked, exposing the company to the sector's inherent cyclicality.
- **New/unproven diversification bets**: both humanoid robotics and aerospace (AS9100D-gated) revenue contributions are still ahead of the company, not yet delivered — execution risk on both fronts.
