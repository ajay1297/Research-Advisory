# Azad Engineering Limited

Azad Engineering Limited manufactures highly-engineered, complex, mission and life-critical forged and machined components — including 3D rotating airfoils and turbine engine parts — for OEMs in the aerospace & defence, energy, and oil & gas industries. Incorporated in 1983 and headquartered in Hyderabad, the company listed on the BSE and NSE (ticker: AZAD) on 28 December 2023 following its IPO at ₹524/share. As of 10 July 2026 it had a market capitalization of approximately ₹16,013 crore (screener.in).

## Value Chain Positioning

Azad is a Tier 1 critical-components supplier sitting between raw-material/alloy inputs and the world's largest aerospace, defence, energy-turbine and oil & gas OEMs. It holds a portfolio of 1,700+ qualified parts across 45+ specialized manufacturing processes, and describes itself as a supplier to six of the key manufacturers in aerospace & defence and five of the key manufacturers in the turbine industry — including customers that together control roughly 75% of the global gas turbine market. ~93% of FY26 standalone revenue was exports, across 12 countries. Downstream, Azad's components go into aircraft engines/structures, gas/steam/nuclear power turbines, and oil & gas drilling/extraction equipment.

## Near Term (Next 1 to 2 Quarters)

- **FY26 Revenue Guidance Exceeded** `[Delivered]`: FY26 standalone revenue grew 30.3% YoY to ~₹590 crore, ahead of the "approximately 25% plus" growth management had previously communicated for the year — the company's highest-ever revenue, EBITDA and PAT. "We delivered our highest ever revenue, highest ever EBITDA and highest ever PAT."

- **Q1 FY27 Revenue Conversion Lag** `[Pending]`: Management flagged the quarter in progress at the time of the call as a "churn-out" period, where output from newly commissioned plants needs time to flow through to dispatch and billed revenue. "the Q1 of this -- the current month quarter, which is going on, this is the churnout month. So the revenues will follow once the metal gets started dispatching."

## Medium Term (6 to 12 Months)

- **FY27 Revenue Growth Guidance Reiterated** `[Pending]`: Management reiterated confidence in sustaining "25% plus" top-line growth for FY27, citing capacity now largely in place, advancing qualifications, and firm customer schedules. "we remain confident in delivering 25% plus top line trajectory, not just for FY '27, but on a multiyear basis."

- **Remaining Dedicated Facilities Completion** `[Pending]`: Of an 8-facility dedicated-OEM buildout, 4 are complete (GE, Siemens, Mitsubishi, Baker Hughes); the remaining facilities under construction/WIP are targeted for completion within FY27. "commissioning the remaining plants that are still under construction or are in WIP stages by the balance of the year, FY '27, with the same discipline that we followed through FY '26."

## Long Term (1+ Years)

- **Order Book Consumption Over 5 Years** `[Pending]`: Azad's order book of ~₹6,500 crore (net of FY26 deliveries, ~11-12x FY26 revenue) is expected to substantially convert to revenue over roughly the next five years. "over the next 5 years, we should be able to more or less consume INR6,500 crores."

- **Nuclear Turbine Expansion (EDF/Arabelle)** `[Pending]`: Azad already supplies nuclear turbine airfoils to EDF's Arabelle Solutions (France) and positions itself to expand in this niche further. "we are one of the only qualified partners in the country to be producing nuclear turbine airfoils. So we are ready and geared up for the opportunity that we are seeing, whether in India or globally."

- **Saudi Arabia Facility (Baker Hughes)** `[Delayed]`: The previously-announced Saudi Arabia facility opportunity linked to Baker Hughes remains open but its timeline has shifted, with management's current bandwidth prioritized on ramping up existing domestic plants. "the time lines have been shifted... that opportunity still lies."

## Marquee & Niche Customers

- **GE Vernova**: Agreement to supply highly engineered, complex rotating and stationary airfoils for Advanced Gas turbine engines; contract valued at USD 112 Mn. (Source: Investor Presentation, May 2026)
- **Honeywell Aerospace**: Single-source supplier partner for highly engineered & complex hot-section Nozzle Vanes Segments of gas turbine engines, under a long-term contract/pricing agreement (LTCPA) signed for 8 years; separately, a Phase 1 business award valued at USD 16 Mn spend over the contract period. (Source: Investor Presentation, May 2026)
- **Mitsubishi Heavy Industries**: LTCPA for supply of complex rotating and stationary airfoils for Advanced Gas & Thermal power turbine engines, valued at USD 83 Mn for a 5-year period. (Source: Investor Presentation, May 2026)
- **Rolls-Royce Plc (London)**: Agreement to produce Civil Aircraft Engine Components and supply super-critical complex machined parts for the life of the engine program; first qualification batch expected in H2 (of the current period), per the Q4 FY26 concall Q&A. (Source: Investor Presentation, May 2026; Q4 FY26 concall, 16 May 2026)
- **EDF / Arabelle Solutions (niche — nuclear)**: Supplies nuclear turbine airfoils to EDF (France), described by management as "the world's largest customer" in this niche; Azad calls itself "one of the only qualified partners in the country" producing nuclear turbine airfoils, supplying for "the last couple of years" after a multi-year audit/qualification process. (Source: Q4 FY26 concall, 16 May 2026)

No customer-concentration percentage (e.g. "top 5/10 customers = X% of revenue") was disclosed in the sources reviewed. **Customers' own guidance**: these are large multinational OEMs (GE, Honeywell, Mitsubishi, Rolls-Royce, EDF) that do not tie public capacity guidance specifically to Azad as a sub-tier supplier; no customer-specific, independently disclosed guidance corroborating demand for Azad's products was found in the sources reviewed for this report. Management did note generically on the call that GE, Mitsubishi and Siemens are "fully booked for next 10 years" — a qualitative demand signal, not a quantified customer disclosure.

## Industry Tailwinds / Headwinds

**Tailwinds**

- **Sharply rising Indian defence budget**: India's defence budget for FY26-27 reached an all-time high (~USD 88bn, +15.2% YoY), with capital expenditure up 21.8% and R&D spending up 8.5% — a direct tailwind for Indian precision-component suppliers to the sector. (Source: sector coverage, 2026)
- **Long order-book visibility across the sub-sector**: sector commentary notes major component suppliers now carry backlogs extending "over a decade," with investor focus shifting from demand generation to delivery capability — consistent with Azad's own ~6,500cr order book. (Source: sector coverage, 2026)
- **Peer momentum**: other Indian precision-manufacturing/aerospace-structures suppliers (e.g. Rossell Techsys, up 87% YoY to ₹485cr revenue in FY26) are also reporting strong growth, indicating a broader sector tailwind rather than an Azad-specific one. (Source: sector coverage, 2026)

**Headwinds**

- **Execution/delivery-capability risk is now the sector's binding constraint**, per the same sector commentary that flags order-book-to-revenue multiples as high across the sub-sector — i.e. the risk has shifted from "can these companies win orders" to "can they convert backlogs to revenue on schedule," directly relevant to Azad's own near-term "churn-out" commentary above.
- **Geopolitical/timeline risk on international capex plans** — directly evidenced by Azad's own Saudi Arabia facility timeline shift (see Long Term outlook above), a risk not unique to Azad among suppliers with cross-border expansion plans.

## Promoter / Governance Track Record

Only two guidance-vs-actual-style items have enough history to assess so far (this being the first research run for this company): FY26 revenue growth (exceeded the ~25%+ guidance) and the Aerospace & Defence ₹100cr milestone (delivered) — both favorable. There is not yet a multi-quarter track record to characterize a broader pattern of beats or misses.

**Shareholding**: Promoter shareholding stood at 55.84% as of March 2026 (screener.in), with FIIs at 14.75%, DIIs at 11.71%, and public at 17.69%. No share pledge was shown in the sources reviewed.

**Promoter Fund Raises**: No promoter/promoter-group preferential allotment, warrant issue, or promoter loan was found in the sources reviewed. One institutional capital raise is on record — a Qualified Institutions Placement (QIP) of ₹700cr (54,68,750 shares at ₹1,280/share) allotted 3 March 2025 to institutional investors, used to fund growth capex (the dedicated OEM facility buildout). This is not a promoter-allottee raise, so it does not carry the same governance-dilution reading as a promoter warrant issue, but is included here for completeness since the CARE rating rationale cites this "planned equity raise" as a supporting factor in the company's liquidity profile.

**Credit Rating Snapshot**: CARE Ratings assigned Azad's long-term bank facilities a first-time rating of A-/Stable in November 2024, upgraded to A/Stable in July 2025, and reaffirmed A/Stable on enhanced facility limits (₹208.32cr, up from ₹145cr) in August 2025 — a positive, improving rating trajectory with no downgrade or negative outlook currently in force. The rating cites experienced promoters, established OEM relationships, and PBILDT margins consistently above 25%, constrained by an elongated working capital cycle and project risk from large debt-funded capex.

**Legal & Litigation**: One matter is confirmed but unquantified. The company's own Investor Presentation (May 2026) footnotes "professional and consultancy charges towards Hamuel litigation" as a non-recurring Adjusted EBITDA add-back (referenced in the FY21 adjustment column) — confirming a litigation matter exists, but no forum, contingent-liability amount, or current status was found in the sources reviewed for this report. No other litigation, tax dispute, or regulatory/arbitration matter was found.

## Key Risks

- **Revenue-conversion/execution risk on the order book**: at ~11-12x FY26 revenue, converting the ~₹6,500cr order book depends on production schedules, capacity ramp-up, and part-by-part qualification status all staying aligned — management's own framing of Q1 FY27 as a "churn-out" quarter where output lags revenue is a live example of this risk.
- **Elongated working capital cycle**: flagged directly by CARE Ratings as a rating constraint, driven by rising receivables and inventory (particularly customised alloys procured in bulk for products under development) — a structural feature of the business given long OEM qualification cycles (30-48 months per part).
- **Project risk from large, debt-funded capex**: also a CARE rating constraint; the company has been investing ahead of demand in new dedicated facilities, which increases leverage and finance costs (borrowings and finance costs both rose materially in FY26) ahead of the associated revenue ramping up.
- **Geopolitical/international-expansion risk**: the Saudi Arabia facility timeline shift illustrates that cross-border capacity plans carry execution risk beyond the company's own operational control.
- **Elevated valuation multiple sensitivity**: at a high forward PE (96.2x, on a growth-rate-derived revenue figure and a trailing-margin assumption), small changes in execution pace, margin trajectory, or the pace of order-book conversion would have an outsized arithmetic effect on this multiple.
- **Unquantified litigation exposure**: the "Hamuel litigation" matter referenced in the company's own investor materials has no disclosed forum, amount, or status in the sources reviewed — worth following up via the annual report's contingent-liabilities note on a future refresh.
