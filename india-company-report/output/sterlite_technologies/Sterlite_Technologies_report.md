# Sterlite Technologies Limited

Sterlite Technologies Limited (STL) was established in July 2001 following the demerger of Sterlite Industries Ltd's telecom division, and describes itself as the largest optical fiber and optical fiber cable manufacturer in India, with a global market presence built over 30+ years, a patent portfolio exceeding 780 filings, and more than 10 zero-waste manufacturing facilities worldwide. It is listed on the BSE (532374) and NSE (STLTECH). As of 10 July 2026 it had a market capitalization of approximately ₹27,907 crore (screener.in).

## Value Chain Positioning

STL manufactures optical fiber and optical fiber cables with full vertical integration "from glass preform to pre-terminated assemblies," selling to telecom operators (Tier 1 operators in India under long-term contracts), global hyperscalers and "neo cloud" data-center providers (predominantly North America), and enterprise customers across Europe, the US and India. A separate business line, STL Digital, provides IT/digital engineering and analytics services. The company avoids the commodity spot market, focusing instead on long-term, often co-developed product partnerships with select customers.

## Industry Tailwinds / Headwinds

**Tailwinds**

- **AI-led data-center buildout**: the dominant structural demand driver cited by management, with ~70% of data-center demand expected to be AI-led by 2030 and hyperscaler capex projected to reach ~USD 762bn (see TAM above).
- **US tariff relief**: US tariff impact on STL is reported to have moderated meaningfully from peak levels, with one Q&A reference to the full impact of a US tariff reduction "from 50% to 15%" still working through results.
- **US BEAD broadband program**: cited by ICRA (in its 7 Jul 2026 rating upgrade) as a tailwind supporting strong US optical fiber demand.
- **North America demand outpacing supply**: per CRU, demand is "consistently outpacing domestic supply in North America, keeping lead times tight" — a favorable pricing/volume dynamic for suppliers with US capacity.

**Headwinds**

- **Geopolitical input-cost pressure**: new near-term cost pressures from the war in West Asia are specifically impacting helium and polymer inputs used in fiber manufacturing.
- **Copper/LME price exposure**: the Enterprise & Data Center segment's FY26 revenue mix moderated due to a decline in the copper business linked to higher LME (London Metal Exchange) copper prices.
- **Germanium constraints**: cited alongside US tariffs as a factor behind stable-but-not-growing ex-China OFC market share (~8% in FY26).
- **Optical connectivity attach-rate moderation**: attach rates fell to 15% in FY26 from 22% in FY25, attributed by management to product mix/project timing and a higher OFC-revenue base — described as temporary, but a real near-term mix headwind.

## Promoter / Governance Track Record

Only one guidance-vs-actual-style item has enough history to assess so far (first research run for this company): FY26 order inflow, which delivered a record ₹7,687cr, more than doubling YoY. There is not yet a multi-quarter track record to characterize a broader pattern, though it's worth noting management explicitly declines to give several categories of forward guidance (utilization, capacity, next-year growth/margin specifics) as a matter of stated policy — this is a communication-style fact, not evidence of unreliability.

**Shareholding**: Promoter shareholding stood at 42.29% as of July 2026 (screener.in), down from 44.44% in March 2026 and from 53.99% in September 2023 — a multi-year declining trend. FII holding rose sharply over the same recent window (11.47% in Mar 2026 to 19.71% in Jul 2026). No share pledge was shown in the sources reviewed, and no explanation for the promoter decline was found — stated without speculation on cause.

**Promoter Fund Raises**: No promoter/promoter-group preferential allotment, warrant issue, or promoter loan was found in the sources reviewed. Management confirmed a "QIP approval" referenced in the Q4 FY26 Q&A is a routine, year-on-year enabling shareholder resolution to preserve capital-raising flexibility, not tied to an identified funding requirement — so this is not logged as an active fund raise.

**Credit Rating Snapshot**: Both agencies covering STL have taken positive rating actions recently. CRISIL revised its outlook to Stable and reaffirmed CRISIL AA-/Stable on bank loan facilities (₹4,045cr) and NCDs (₹390cr), and CRISIL A1+ on commercial paper (₹800cr), on 16 June 2026 — citing the Q4 FY26 turnaround, the order backlog, and the USD 1.1bn hyperscaler contract, with net leverage improving from 3.2x to 1.8x and interest coverage from 1.9x to 2.9x. ICRA then upgraded STL's long-term ratings from AA- to AA on 7 July 2026 (short-term ratings reaffirmed). No downgrade or negative/watch-negative outlook is currently in force from either agency — however, **both agencies' own rationales explicitly flag the pending Prysmian trade-secret litigation as a live risk factor**, discussed in full below.

**Legal & Litigation**: Two material matters are on record, with opposite outcomes.
1. **Prysmian Group vs. Sterlite Technologies Inc. (trade secret misappropriation)** — a South Carolina jury awarded Prysmian USD96.5mn (~₹800cr+) on 9 August 2024, after Prysmian alleged a former executive who joined Sterlite in 2020 took confidential customer, product and manufacturing-expansion information. The District Court affirmed the verdict on 29 August 2025 (while denying Prysmian's request for prejudgment interest). Sterlite has publicly stated its intent to pursue further appeal; the sources reviewed do not show a final appellate resolution as of July 2026. **This should be read as an adverse judgment currently under appeal, not a closed or dismissed matter** — both CRISIL and ICRA's own rating rationales continue to flag it as an active risk.
2. **Sterlite Technologies vs. Fujikura (European patent dispute, EP 3796060)** — the EPO Technical Board of Appeal revoked Fujikura's patent in its entirety on 24 June 2026, a final and non-appealable ruling in STL's favor, resolving related UK litigation over STL's Celesta cable family in STL's favor. STL is now pursuing recovery of its legal defence costs. This is a favorable outcome, not a risk.

## Key Risks

- **The Prysmian USD96.5mn adverse verdict is the single largest disclosed risk item covered in this report series to date.** At ~₹800cr+, it is roughly 1.3x STL's FY26 EBITDA (₹628cr) and over 14x FY26 PAT (₹56cr) — a verdict of this scale, if ultimately upheld through appeal, would be financially material relative to STL's current earnings base. The company is appealing, and the final outcome and timeline are not yet known from the sources reviewed.
- **Explicit management non-disclosure on forward guidance, utilization and capacity**: management has stated it will not provide next-year growth/margin guidance, specific utilization figures, or post-capex capacity figures — this is a legitimate communication choice but means investors have less forward visibility from STL directly than from some peers in this report series, and this report's Forward PE section was omitted as a direct consequence.
- **Input-cost and geopolitical exposure**: helium and polymer cost pressure from the West Asia conflict, and germanium constraints, are both live near-term cost headwinds on the core fiber-manufacturing business.
- **Segment-mix dependency on the Enterprise & Data Center ramp**: the FY27 target of scaling this segment to 30% of revenue (from 19%) depends on continued hyperscaler capex and successful execution of the ~USD 1.1bn framework agreement — a single large, concentrated commercial relationship.
- **Copper/commodity price exposure**: the copper business's sensitivity to LME pricing has already dragged on Enterprise & Data Center segment revenue once in FY26 and remains a live exposure.
- **Declining promoter holding**: down from 53.99% (Sep 2023) to 42.29% (Jul 2026), with no disclosed explanation found in the sources reviewed.
- **Extreme recent share-price appreciation** (+410% over one year) increases the stock's sensitivity to any negative surprise, including on the Prysmian appeal, even though this is a market-price observation rather than a fundamental risk to the business itself.
