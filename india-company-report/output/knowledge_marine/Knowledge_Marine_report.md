# Knowledge Marine & Engineering Works Limited

Knowledge Marine & Engineering Works Limited provides dredging services and owns/operates marine craft, alongside repair, maintenance and refitting of marine crafts and marine infrastructure, operating in India and Myanmar. Incorporated in 2015, the company's fleet has grown from 3 dredgers and 5 port ancillary crafts (March 2021) to 12 dredgers (including 3 trailing suction hopper dredgers and 7 cutter suction dredgers) and 16 port ancillary crafts (March 2025). It is listed on the BSE and NSE. As of 10 July 2026 it had a market capitalization of approximately ₹5,935 crore (screener.in).

## Value Chain Positioning

Knowledge Marine operates as an owner-operator across three related maritime verticals: dredging services (capital and maintenance dredging for ports/waterways), charter hire of marine craft (including a growing green-tug fleet), and shipbuilding (constructing vessels, increasingly at its own new Saphale shipyard). Its customers are predominantly government port authorities and maritime bodies — named counterparties include V.O. Chidambaranar Port Authority and Visakhapatnam Port. The company is entering shipbuilding for government-backed green-tug programmes, benefiting from Government of India shipbuilding subsidy support and a newly-approved Tonnage Tax Scheme.

## Medium Term (6 to 12 Months)

- **FY27 EBITDA Margin Guidance** `[Pending]`: Management guided EBITDA margins to remain in the 35-40% range for FY27, assuming continued Government of India shipbuilding subsidy inflow; without the subsidy, shipbuilding-specific margins would run 25-30%. "So our EBITDA margins will continue to remain between 35% to 40%."

- **Tonnage Tax Regime Now in Effect** `[Pending]`: The company's dredging/chartering income now falls under the newly-approved 10-year Tonnage Tax Scheme (effective from AY2026-27), while shipbuilding income remains under normal corporate tax — a structural change expected to keep the group's overall effective tax rate low. "chartering and dredging business, which will fall under the tonnage tax scheme... doing the shipbuilding activity will fall under corporate tax."

## Industry Tailwinds / Headwinds

**Tailwinds**

- **Government push toward green/coastal maritime infrastructure**: the Green Tug Transition Programme (₹650cr of contracts already secured) reflects a broader government-backed push toward emission-reduction in port operations, a structural multi-year demand driver.
- **Government shipbuilding subsidy support**: directly cited by management as the swing factor between 25-30% and 35-40% EBITDA margins in the shipbuilding segment — a policy tailwind if sustained.
- **Niche positioning versus DCI**: management frames the shallow-draft/smaller-vessel dredging niche as underserved by the larger Dredging Corporation of India, which is investing in larger cutter-suction/water-injection dredgers instead — reducing direct competitive overlap in Knowledge Marine's core segment.

**Headwinds**

- **Aging core fleet**: CARE Ratings flags the trailing suction hopper dredger (TSHD) fleet's average age at over 50 years, entailing potential maintenance/replacement capex risk.
- **Order-book concentration**: top three orders account for 60% of the order book per CARE Ratings — a structural dependency on a small number of large, long-duration contracts.
- **Working-capital-intensive operations**: flagged by CARE as a rating constraint, characterized by high gross current asset days.

## Key Risks

- **Order-book concentration**: CARE Ratings' own flag that the top three orders make up 60% of the order book means the loss, delay, or non-renewal of even one or two large contracts (including the 15-year Green Tug awards) would have an outsized impact.
- **Aging TSHD fleet**: an average trailing suction hopper dredger age of over 50 years (per CARE) implies latent maintenance/replacement capex risk not yet reflected in current capex guidance.
- **Working-capital intensity**: CARE-flagged high gross current asset days is a structural constraint on liquidity as the company scales.
- **Tax-regime dependency**: FY26's PAT margin (30.9%) is materially elevated by the new Tonnage Tax Scheme; this is a real, approved structural benefit but means historical PAT margins prior to the scheme are not a reliable base for projecting forward margins, and any future change to tonnage-tax eligibility or rules would be a direct earnings risk.
- **Execution risk on Green Tug/shipbuilding ramp**: shipbuilding is a newer capability for the company (constructing at its own yard for the first time at Saphale); cost or schedule overruns on the 15-year Green Tug contracts would affect both revenue timing and the blended margin profile.
- **Moderate scale and cyclicality**: CARE explicitly notes KMEWL's "moderate scale of operations" and dependence on the shipping/maritime industry's inherent cyclicality and regulatory risk.
- **Declining promoter holding**: down ~13.5 percentage points over three years (67.09% to 53.63%), with no disclosed explanation found in the sources reviewed.
