# Karnika Industries Ltd

Karnika Industries Limited (NSE SME: KARNIKA), incorporated in 2017 and based in Howrah, West Bengal, is an ISO 14001:2015 certified manufacturer, trader, and exporter of children's readymade garments — tops, dresses, denims, leggings, skirts, and ponchos for infants through age 14 — sold B2B across 28 states through 150+ distribution partners and 2,500 clients. The company acquired a strategic stake in Kidcity, a kids' retail and D2C brand, during FY26 to expand beyond manufacturing into shop-in-shop retail and omnichannel e-commerce. Karnika delivered FY26 consolidated revenue of INR248 crore and carries a market capitalization of roughly INR742-786 crore.

## Value Chain Positioning

Karnika is a B2B garment manufacturer/job-worker, not a brand-facing retailer, on the Karnika side of the business: it buys yarn/fabric (management says availability is not a bottleneck — "Yarn is available. You can get it as much as you want") and outsources part of the cut-and-sew process to job workers rather than manufacturing 100% in-house, then sells finished kidswear to wholesalers, commission agents, and large-format value retail chains (Style Bazaar, Bumzy, Hopscotch), who in turn distribute PAN-India to end consumers. With the FY26 Kidcity stake acquisition, the group now also owns a downstream D2C/retail layer directly — Kidcity operates its own shop-in-shop counters, EBOs, and marketplace listings (FirstCry, Ajio, Flipkart, Amazon, its own site) — so Karnika now spans from mid-chain manufacturing (its original position) to front-end retail/D2C (via Kidcity), rather than remaining a pure upstream supplier.

## Near Term (Next 1 to 2 Quarters)

- **Working Capital Cycle Cut Further** `[On Track]`: Management has already reduced the working capital cycle from 4.5-5 months to about 4 months and is targeting a further ~100-day reduction in the current financial year — the first leg of this reduction is already realized, not just promised. "Last year our cycle is around four and half to five months. Current financial year we have reduced it to around four months and we are planning to reduce it by around 100 days in the coming financial year."

- **Kiosk and EBO Payback Periods** `[Pending]`: New Kidcity kiosks are expected to pay back in 8-9 months and exclusive brand outlets (EBOs) in 15-18 months, the near-term unit economics underpinning the retail rollout. First disclosure of these specific paybacks; no store cohort has yet completed a full payback cycle to confirm them. "Kiosk, as I told, it will be around eight to nine months. EBOs will be around 15 to 18 months."

- **Cotton Cost Pressure Easing** `[On Track]`: The elevated cotton-price pressure that weighed on Q4 FY26 margins has largely normalized entering the new financial year, per management — an already-observed improvement, not a forward promise. "It was there in quarter four, but now it's quite normalized."

## Medium Term (6 to 12 Months)

- **Kidcity Revenue to Roughly Triple** `[Pending]`: Management projects Kidcity's standalone revenue to roughly 3x in FY27 from the ~INR24-25 crore it delivered in FY26, against an original FY26 guide of INR30 crore. Worth flagging: this is a fresh FY27 target from a business unit that missed its own FY26 target by ~18% (see Promoter/Governance below) — the higher multiple asked of FY27 makes this more demanding to hit, not less. "I think it would be, we project around 3x revenue from the Kidcity for next financial year."

- **Karnika Standalone Growth Guidance** `[Pending]`: Karnika's standalone business is guided to grow 25-30% in the near-to-medium term, self-funded from internal earnings, while Kidcity's separate expansion needs are being funded via the promoter-backed warrant raise. First disclosure of this specific range. "In Karnika standalone, it will be around 25% to 30%, but Kidcity will show a major growth pattern. This will increase way more than that."

- **Consolidated Margins to Normalize** `[Pending]`: Management expects consolidated PAT margins to settle in an 11-13% band over the next two to three years as Kidcity integration and transitional costs fade. First disclosure of this specific range. "Normalized margins would be around, if I consolidate the balance sheet, that will be in a range of around 11% to 13%."

## Long Term (1+ Years)

- **FY28 Combined Revenue Target** `[On Track]`: Management has guided to a combined Karnika + Kidcity revenue target of roughly INR425 crore by FY28, versus INR248 crore delivered in FY26 — consistent with the 30-35% CAGR framing management has reiterated on the subsequent call. "For '28, we are targeting for 42,500 lakhs."

- **Kidcity Scaling to INR200-250cr** `[On Track]`: By FY28-29, management expects Kidcity alone to reach INR200-250 crore in revenue as its shop-in-shop and EBO network scales into high-street malls and airports — a target *raised* from the INR120 crore (Rs12,000 lakh) by FY28 originally guided at the H1 FY26 call, even though Kidcity's own FY26 delivery fell short of plan. "If I go by the financial year ‘28 or ‘29, it will be definitely around INR200 crores to INR250 crores revenue from the Kidcity." — vs. the original "Kidcity's financial outlook for the next three years shows revenue growth from Rs. 3,000 lakhs in FY'26 to Rs. 12,000 lakhs by FY28" (H1 FY26 concall, Nov 2025)

- **30-35% Multi-Year CAGR Target** `[On Track]`: Management is guiding to a 30-35% consolidated revenue CAGR over the next three to four years, positioning Kidcity's integrated shop-in-shop format in tier 2/3 malls as a category no organized peer currently occupies — a figure consistent with, and reiterating, the FY28 combined target above. "We are making CAGR of around 30% to 35% in the next three to four years."

## Marquee & Niche Customers

- **Style Bazaar, Bumzy, Hopscotch**: Named large-format/value retail chains from which Karnika secured fresh orders during H1 FY26. "Like we have secured order from Style Bazaar, Bumzy, Hopscotch. These all orders we have taken from them in H1." (H1 FY26 concall, Nov 2025)
- **Landmark Group and Lulu (Middle East, targeted, not yet confirmed live)**: Management named these as specific large Middle East retail chains it is pursuing as it shifts from wholesaler-only to direct value-retail-chain relationships in the region — a pipeline item, not a confirmed existing customer. "Like Landmark Group and Lulu. These are the quite big supply chains in Middle East countries. So, we are targeting for those supply chains also." (H1 FY26 concall, Nov 2025)
- **HopScotch**: Described by Kidcity co-founder Jay Varma as "India's second largest marketplace for kidswear", alongside live listings on FirstCry, Ajio, Flipkart, Amazon, and Kidcity's own D2C site — the omnichannel backbone of the Kidcity growth story. "predominantly available on HopScotch, which is India's second largest marketplace for kidswear." (H1 FY26 concall, Nov 2025)

Customer concentration: management stated no single customer relationship carries a materially different margin ("We all have the same customer margin. Not more than 19%-20%" — H1 FY26 concall), and separately that Karnika runs roughly 100 repeat B2B customers with only ~10% new-customer addition per year and 90%+ revenue from long-standing repeat clients. On the May 2026 call, management added that the top 10 commission-agent relationships (through which PAN-India end-customers are billed) contribute 60-65% of that clientele's revenue — see Key Risks for the concentration angle on this.

**Customer's own guidance, independently checked**: Hopscotch has itself publicly disclosed an ambition to scale 10x in 5 years with double-digit EBITDA, on ~USD71 million raised to date, and specifically flagged 2026 store expansion into East India — Kolkata, Visakhapatnam, and Patna — the same Kolkata-centric market Karnika itself calls its home base. This is independent, third-party corroboration of demand for the marketplace relationship described above. No independently disclosed guidance or expansion plans were found for Style Bazaar or Bumzy in the sources reviewed (both appear to be smaller/private retail chains with no public disclosures). Landmark Group and Lulu are not yet confirmed customers (see above), so a guidance check was not performed for them.

## Capacity Utilization & Headroom

Management gave two utilization figures within the same H1 FY26 concall: "Currently, we are utilizing around 80%-85%" (CFO, earlier in the call) and, later, "Utilization is at 90% now" (MD, in response to a direct follow-up) — both are reproduced here rather than reconciled into one, since the second may reflect a more current/specific answer to the same underlying question. The figures below use the higher, more specific 90% reading against Karnika-standalone FY26 revenue (~INR223.5cr, i.e. INR248cr consolidated less Kidcity's ~INR24.5cr).

| | Revenue | Basis |

**Flag: utilization is already at/above the 85% threshold** — management itself confirms this is a binding constraint, not a computed artifact: existing in-house space is "fully occupied," a rented additional space has already been taken, and management states plainly it will "go to the market" for more space to hit its >INR300cr standalone target over the next 2-3 years. Near-term growth beyond ~INR248cr (standalone) depends on the capacity additions in the timeline above, not further utilization gains from what already exists.

## Industry Tailwinds / Headwinds

**Tailwinds**

- **Organized-retail shift**: India's kidswear market continues to shift from unorganized to organized/branded retail, driven by rising disposable incomes, urbanization, and growing e-commerce and tier-2/3 penetration — a structural tailwind for scaled, quality-focused players. (Source: IMARC Group, India Kids Apparel Market report, 2026)
- **Large but slower-growing TAM**: The India kidswear market is estimated at ~USD11.1 billion in 2025, projected to reach ~USD17.4 billion by 2034 (~4.9-5% CAGR) — a large addressable market, but one growing far slower than Karnika's own 30-35% company-level target, implying the company must be taking share rather than simply riding industry growth. (Source: IMARC Group / industry estimates cited on the May 2026 concall)

**Headwinds**

- **Cotton/input-cost inflation**: Domestic cotton and yarn prices rose roughly 4-8% in early 2026 (Shankar-6 cotton up ~8.5% month-on-month), with the expiry of an 11% import duty waiver on cotton at end-December 2025 pushing domestic prices higher — an industry-wide cost pressure that corroborates Karnika management's own Q4 FY26 margin commentary. (Source: Apparel Resources / Fibre2Fashion cotton-yarn price reporting, 2026)
- **Export/geopolitical exposure**: Management flagged Gulf-region tensions as a watch item for the ~5% of sales that come from exports, though it does not currently see this as a material near-term margin risk.

## Key Risks

- **Share-count / thin-float effect on market reaction**: Q1 FY27 standalone profit more than doubled year-on-year, yet basic EPS fell to ₹1.19 from ₹2.48 due to the ~5x increase in paid-up equity capital, and the stock fell ~18% in a single day following the results — a reminder that headline profit growth alone did not satisfy the market once share-count effects and a thin free float were factored in. (Financial/market risk)
- **Pending promoter warrant conversion**: INR48cr of promoter/promoter-group warrants remain pending conversion within an 18-month window (by ~December 2027). Conversion would add ~39.67 lakh shares to an already-enlarged base; a lapse would mean promoters forfeit the 25% already paid — worth tracking on every refresh. (Governance risk)
- **Kidcity integration execution risk**: FY26 Kidcity revenue delivery (~INR24-25cr) missed the original guidance of INR30cr by roughly 18%, even as the combined Karnika + Kidcity number beat guidance. Management's FY27 (3x) and FY28 (INR200-250cr) targets for Kidcity require materially better execution than FY26 delivered. (Business/execution risk)
- **Input-cost exposure**: Cotton and yarn prices are rising industry-wide, a direct input cost to Karnika's own manufacturing margins, per both management commentary and independent industry reporting. (Financial/macro risk)
- **Customer concentration via intermediaries**: Roughly 60-65% of revenue flows through the top 10 commission-agent relationships in Kolkata; while management describes end-consumers as broad-based repeat clients, billing concentration in a small number of intermediary agents is a structural dependency worth flagging. (Business risk)
- **Capacity constraint on near-term growth**: Manufacturing utilization is already at ~90% per management's own most recent statement, with existing in-house space "fully occupied" and a rented overflow space already taken up; current capacity alone supports only ~INR248cr (standalone), leaving ~11% headroom before management's own >INR300cr target requires new space it has said it will need to "go to the market" for. (Business/execution risk)
- **Unquantified pending litigation**: The IPO prospectus discloses ongoing legal proceedings involving the Company and a Group Company, without public detail on nature, forum, or amount available in the sources reviewed — a known gap worth closing via the DRHP or a recent annual report before treating this as immaterial. (Governance risk)
